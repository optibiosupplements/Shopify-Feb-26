The Chandrasekhar 2012 study

In 2012, a team led by Dr. K. Chandrasekhar set out to answer a simple question: can a specific ashwagandha extract actually reduce cortisol — the primary stress hormone — in people with chronic stress?†

The setup was rigorous:

Design Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Participants 64 adults with a history of chronic stress
Duration 60 days
Dose 600mg KSM-66® daily (two 300mg capsules)
Measurement Serum cortisol via blood test + PSS questionnaire

This wasn't a customer survey or a marketing study. They drew blood. They measured cortisol in a lab. And here's what they found:

27.9% reduction in serum cortisol levels.†

Chandrasekhar et al., 2012 — PubMed ID: 23439798

44% reduction in perceived stress scores (PSS).†

Same study — validated Perceived Stress Scale

Two different measurement methods. Two clear, statistically significant results. One ingredient — at 600mg/day.

 

What does 27.9% cortisol reduction actually feel like?

Numbers on a lab report don't mean much in daily life. But here's what chronically elevated cortisol feels like — and what happens when it comes back to healthy levels:

High cortisol Healthy cortisol
Wired at night, exhausted by morning Naturally tired at bedtime, alert in the morning

This isn't about eliminating stress from your life — that's impossible. It's about supporting your body's ability to respond to stress in a healthy, proportional way.†
